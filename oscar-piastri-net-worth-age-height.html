Biography of Oscar Piastri

Oscar Jack Piastri, an accomplished Australian race car driver, has established himself in the world of motorsport. He recently signed on with McLaren to compete in the 2023 Formula One World Championship as a driver. Prior to that, he participated in the Alpine F1 Team’s driving training and served as the team’s backup driver.

Piastri’s accomplishments in the simpler formulae are very amazing. With R-ace GP, he won the 2019 Formula Renault Eurocup. Then, with Prema Racing, he won the 2020 FIA Formula 3 Championship and the 2021 Formula 2 Championship.

He accomplished a remarkable accomplishment by becoming the fifth driver to win the GP3/Formula 3 Championship and the GP2/Formula 2 Championship in successive seasons, as well as the sixth driver to win the GP2/Formula 2 Championship in their debut season. He has established a reputation as one of the sport’s most promising young drivers thanks to his remarkable achievements.

Piastri, a Melbourne native, began his racing career with remote-control vehicles before switching to karting in 2011. He entered a number of Australian events and championships after turning professional in 2014.

He started participating in CIK-FIA-sanctioned European and other karting competitions the following year with Ricky Flynn Motorsport. He relocated to the UK in 2016 to advance his racing career, and he placed sixth in the Bahrain World Championship that year.

Early in 2016, Piastri secured his first significant sponsorship from his father’s HP Tuners, which helped support his racing career. Throughout the GP3 Series, F3, and F2 seasons, this sponsorship was clearly visible on his racing gear and vehicle. During that year, he made his single-seater debut in a few Formula 4 UAE Championship rounds with Dragon F4, winning two podiums on route to a sixth-place finish.

Piastri joins the lineup of the TRS Arden Junior Racing Team for the British F4 championship in 2017. With six victories and six pole positions, he dazzled everyone and came in second place to Jamie Caroline.

Red Bull Racing CEO Christian Horner admitted that he lost the opportunity to bring Piastri to their junior squad during his Formula 4 years in September 2022.
