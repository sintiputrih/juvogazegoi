How did Feliks Zemdegs start his Professional Career?

Feliks Zemdegs’ professional career began in April 2008 when he purchased his first speed cube, motivated by YouTube tutorials and videos about speedcubing. On June 14, 2008, he recorded his first casual time, which was an average of 19.73 seconds.

Since he was twelve years old, he has been solving the 3×3×3 with CFOP, the 4×4×4 with the Yau technique, the 2×2×2 with the CLL method, and the 5×5×5 through 7×7×7 with the Reduction method.

His first world records for the 3×3×3 average and the 4×4×4 average were achieved on January 30, 2010, at the Melbourne Summer Open, in times of 9.21 seconds and 42.01 seconds, respectively. From that point until April 23, 2017, he held the 3x3x3 world record continuously, finally beating it by eight times to 6.45 seconds.
